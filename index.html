🎴 Pokemon TCG Price Prediction Model

4 pillars · Pokemon · Art · Supply saturation · Demand pressure → predicted price

📖 Tutorial — Complete Guide

Section 1 — Setting Up MyCollectrics Fetching (One-Time Setup)

  1. Install Node.js if you don't have it — download from nodejs.org (use the LTS version).
  2. Open Terminal (Mac) or Command Prompt (Windows).
  3. Navigate to this folder. On Mac:
    cd "/Users/yourname/Desktop/price prediction model pokemon"
  4. Install Puppeteer (this downloads a headless browser that MyCollectrics needs):
    npm install puppeteer
    This takes about 1–3 minutes the first time.
  5. Start the proxy server:
    node server.js
    You should see ✅ Pokemon TCG Price Proxy running at http://localhost:3001.
  6. Leave that Terminal window open while using this model. Closing it stops the proxy.
  7. Refresh this page. The status above should change from 🔴 Proxy OFF to 🟢 Local proxy ON.

Section 2 — Predicting a Single Card's Price

  1. In the Card Input panel on the left, select the Set from the dropdown (e.g. "Twilight Masquerade").
  2. Pick a Rarity. The card dropdown below populates with every card in that set and rarity.
  3. Pick a specific Card. The card image appears below it.
  4. Click 📊 MyCollectrics to auto-fill: current price, demand pressure, supply saturation, gem rate, and PSA 10 population. A price-history chart appears under the image.
  5. Adjust the Art tier (slider 0–100) based on how visually striking the artwork is.
  6. Click 🎯 Auto next to the Pokemon tier dropdown — it will infer the tier from the card name automatically.
  7. If the card is graded, pick the grade (e.g. "PSA 10 Gem Mint") from the Grade dropdown.
  8. Check any active catalysts (anniversary year, Pokemon Presents announcement, reprint risk, etc.).
  9. Click 📊 Predict. The right panel shows: predicted price, confidence band, classification (ELITE / CHASE / STRONG / MID / WEAK), and projections for +1 month / +3 months / +6 months / +1 year.

Section 3 — Building Your Portfolio

  1. After predicting a card, click ➕ Add to portfolio to track it.
  2. In the Portfolio table, fill in the Paid column for each card so the model can compute your Profit / Loss.
  3. When you sell a card, fill in the Sold column. The card stays in the portfolio but is flagged as sold.
  4. Click 🔄 Refresh prices periodically to pull the latest market prices and update predictions.
  5. Click 📥 Import Collectr CSV to bulk-import an existing collection from Collectr's CSV export.
  6. Click 💾 Backup all (JSON) regularly to save your data — localStorage can be cleared.

Section 4 — Calibration (Making the Model More Accurate)

  1. For cards you've sold or know the actual market price of, fill in the Actual column in the portfolio table.
  2. Aim for at least 15 cards with real sold prices before calibrating.
  3. Click 🔬 Calibrate weights. The model grid-searches across pillar weights to minimize prediction error.
  4. Review the suggested new weights. If they look reasonable, click Apply. Otherwise click Cancel.
  5. Calibration improves the model's accuracy for your specific market preferences.

Section 5 — Simulation Mode (Paper Trading)

  1. Scroll to the 🎮 Simulation Mode panel.
  2. Click 🎯 Start / Reset and enter starting capital (e.g. S$10,000).
  3. Predict any card normally, then click 🎮 Simulation Buy instead of Add to portfolio. The model snapshots all predictions at buy time.
  4. Come back days or weeks later, click 🔄 Refresh prices in the simulation panel.
  5. The Verdict column shows whether the model's prediction at that elapsed time matched reality — ✅ on target, 📈 beat by X%, or 📉 below by X%.
  6. This is your validation loop — over a few weeks, you'll see whether the model is genuinely useful or just guessing.

Section 6 — Pack Expected Value Calculator

  1. Pick a Set in the Card Input panel.
  2. Click 📦 Pack Expected Value.
  3. Enter the pack cost in SGD (most modern packs are S$7–12).
  4. The model fetches sample prices from each chase rarity in the set and computes the Expected Value per pack.
  5. Verdict — if Expected Value > Pack Cost, ripping is profitable on average. If less, buy singles instead.

Section 7 — Understanding the Pillars

  • Pillar 1 — Art tier (0–100): 0 = minimalist, 50 = detailed, 100 = painterly. The most heavily weighted identity pillar (24%) because art drives chase prices.
  • Pillar 2 — Pokemon tier (1–4): Tier 1 is the iconic six (Pikachu, Charizard, etc.). Tier 4 is niche / trainers.
  • Pillar 3 — Demand pressure (0–15%): From MyCollectrics — percentage of listings selling per day. Higher = tighter market.
  • Pillar 4 — Supply saturation shift (0–2): From MyCollectrics — 7-day vs 30-day baseline. Below 1.0 means tightening, above means loosening.
  • Pillar 5 — PSA 10 population: Total PSA 10 copies in existence. Smaller = scarcer = higher price.
  • Pillar 6 — Population growth rate (% per year): How fast new PSA 10 copies are being graded. Fast growth = future supply increase = price pressure.

Section 8 — Classification Labels

Composite score (weighted sum of all pillars) maps to a classification:

  • ELITE (composite ≥ 0.80): top-tier chase, projected to appreciate strongly.
  • CHASE (≥ 0.65): solid chase card with good fundamentals.
  • STRONG (≥ 0.50): meaningful long-term holder.
  • MID (≥ 0.35): middle-of-the-pack card. Limited upside.
  • WEAK (< 0.35): bulk / unwanted. Flat at best.

Tips and Best Practices

  • Use the Confidence score as your trust meter. HIGH means the prediction has real data behind it; LOW means it's mostly a guess.
  • For cards from very new sets, the Pokemon TCG API may not have prices yet — use MyCollectrics or enter the price manually.
  • Refresh prices weekly to keep your portfolio current and feed the similarity-matching model with fresh history.
  • Back up your data (💾 Backup all) before clearing browser cache or switching devices.
  • Pokemon prices are sticky — predictions almost never project sharp drops unless a clear hype correction is detected.
